Other Useful Links

Gregorian Chant

Here are some helpful resources for Gregorian chant:

  • Gregorian Chant Hymns

    Gregorian Chant Hymns has an extensive collection of sheet music and recordings of popular plainchant for the liturgy.

  • Reading & Singing Gregorian Chant

    Corpus Christi Watershed offers eleven lessons for learning how to read and sing Gregorian chant.

  • Pronunciation of Ecclesiastical Latin

    Here is a document outlining correct Ecclesiastical Latin pronunciation as standardised by Pope Pius X.

  • Jubilate Deo

    Pope Paul VI sent a copy of Jubilate Deo to every bishop in April 1974, containing the minimum plainchant repertoire the faithful should know.

  • Square Note: Gregorian Chant App

    A free app containing notation for the most common chants of the Mass: Ordinaries, Propers, and occasional hymns. Tapping a note plays the melody, helping singers to learn the chants.

Church Documents on Sacred Music

Here are the most relevant Church documents on sacred music:

  • Tra le Sollecitudini

    Issued by Pope St Pius X in 1903, Tra le Sollecitudini helped revive Gregorian chant and explains sacred music.

  • Musicae Sacrae Disciplina

    The 1955 encyclical of Pope Pius XII, Musicae sacrae disciplina, reaffirms the duty to preserve Latin plainchant and sacred music.

  • De Musica Sacra

    The Sacred Congregation of Rites issued De musica sacra in 1958, addressing amplification, automatic instruments, and pre-recorded music.

  • Sacrosanctum Concilium

    Sacrosanctum concilium was the first Second Vatican Council document on liturgy; Chapter VI focuses on sacred music.

  • Musicam Sacram

    Issued in 1967, Musicam sacram addresses vernacular use, Gregorian chant, instruments, and text hierarchy in the liturgy.
